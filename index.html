Gaurav Rattan

Assistant Professor in Applied Math, University of Twente (NL)


My research is focused on graphs and networks, mainly,

  • Algorithms, Logic and Complexity of Graph Problems
    • Graph Isomorphism, Graph Homomorphisms
  • Machine Learning on Graphs, Theory and Applications
    • Theoretical Foundations of Graph Neural Networks

Please find linked: my Google Scholar page and my LinkedIn page.


My research has received generous support from

  • an NWO VIDI Talent grant (2025– )
    • Learning on Graphs: Symmetry Meets Structure (LOGSMS)
  • a DFG Eigene Stelle grant (2019–2022)
    • Frameworks for Measuring Graph Similarity

I am also a participant in the Nationaal Groeifonds POLARIS consortium, within the Optimization module (led by Marc Uetz).


Brief Bio. I joined the DMMP group at the University of Twente as an Assistant Professor in May 2024. Previously, I was a postdoc at TU Darmstadt (hosted by Pascal Schweitzer) and a DFG Eigene Stelle researcher at RWTH Aachen (hosted by Martin Grohe). I obtained my PhD degree in 2017 at The Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai (advised by V. Arvind). Much earlier, I was an undergraduate at IIT Bombay.
